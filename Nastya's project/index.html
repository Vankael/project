Martin Luther

Martin Luther was born on November 10, 1483, in Eisleben, Germany, into a middle-class family. His father, Hans Luther, was a miner who worked hard to provide a better future for his son. Wanting Martin to become a lawyer, his father enrolled him at the University of Erfurt, where he earned a master’s degree in 1505. However, Luther’s life changed dramatically that same year.

John Calvin

John Calvin was born on July 10, 1509, Noyon, Picardy, was a theologian and ecclesiastical statesman. He was the leading French Protestant reformer and the most important figure in the second generation of the Protestant Reformation.

Impact on Church & Society

The impact of the 95 Theses was enormous. It challenged the authority of the Catholic Church, led to the formation of new Christian denominations, and contributed to religious and political upheaval across Europe. The Reformation’s effects can still be seen today in the diversity of Christian beliefs and practices around the world..
